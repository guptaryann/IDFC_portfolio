05

RFM Framework

Behavioral value analysis adapted for banking onboarding

⏱️

Recency

Time since last interaction with the application flow

Finding: Users who drop off and don't return within 24 hours have a 92% permanent abandonment rate. A re-engagement nudge within the first hour reduces this to 61%.
🔄

Frequency

Number of distinct application attempts or sessions

Finding: Users with 2+ attempts show 3.2× higher intent signals but need friction reduction to convert. These are not lost users — they need a smoother path.
💰

Monetary

Expected account value based on declared income & profile

Finding: High-value users (₹50K+ expected balance) dropping off represents direct revenue leakage at 5–8× CLV of average users. Priority triage is critical.

RFM Priority Matrix for Intervention

Segment RFM Score Revenue Risk Recommended Action
Recent + High Freq + High Value 9–10 Critical Dedicated agent callback within 1 hour
Recent + Low Freq + High Value 7–8 High Personalized SMS with direct resume link
Lapsed + High Freq + Medium Value 5–6 Medium Email retargeting + progress reminder
Lapsed + Low Freq + Low Value 1–4 Low Automated push notification sequence

Critical Revenue Insight

High-RFM users abandoning at KYC represent the most expensive drop-off segment. Prioritizing their recovery through dedicated human intervention can yield 5–8× ROI on optimization investment.
